Launch

The launch of a rocket is a very highly coordinated process. We have the ground team, mission control and resources to ensure a safe and smooth launch. Our team will ensure that all systems are functioning properly as a "go" for launch. The space ship is chosen by our team based on the time of the year, pricing plan that you chose, and the number you people you have with you.

Our team will make any final adjustments to ensure that you and your crew will have a safe flight. This includes proper fitting of the carbon-fiber suit, perfect communications with Mission control and a physical check.

After the astronauts enter the spacecraft and are safely secured in their seats, our mission control will go through a rundown of pre-launch procedures to ensure that the rocket will be safe for launch

Stage Separation

As the rocket still ascends in the atmosphere, the first stage and the second stage boosters burn through until it is extinguished. You will be thrilled at the black skies and the blue Earth through the window.

At T+2 minutes, the first stage cuts off and separates from the rocket body, which will autonomously and on a landing pad.

At T+10 minutes, the second stage cuts off from the crew capsule and descends back downward to another launchpad on Earth. The crew capsule will then orbit around Earth and stay until it is given the green light to slingshot to the Moon.

Experiencing the delight

Once the mission is given a go for launch by AstroTours Mission Control, the rocket will slingshot from Earth’s gravity field towards a trajectory to the lunar surface. At this point, you can unbuckle your seatbelt and float out of your seat with no gravity. The rocket interface system is designed for your enjoyment during the trip: windows that encompass breathtaking views of the Earth and the Moon, automatic air conditioning and already-prepared delicious space food.

The rocket capsule will orbit around the Moon and release a lander vehicle that will take you down to the Moon’s surface. Our services have provided the best possible availability for you to enjoy your visit to the Moon. For instance, our new state-of-the-art Moon buggy is designed for you and your crewmates to comfortably traverse the lunar surface!

Returning

After your relaxing visit, the capsule will boost and autonomously determine a trajectory towards Earth and boost at full speed. While you may be exhausted during the buggy ride, our relaxing and flexible carbon fiber chairs will soothe your experience back home.

Prior to recovery, AstroTours will check the splashdown site conditions. Our Mission Control team will assign a primary and secondary splashdown 2 days before and determine if each site is a “Go” or a “No-go”. In addition, AstroTours will evaluate the available rescue teams that will drag the astronauts out of the crew capsule. The crew must be in liftoff position and embrace the heat shields while descending.

Recovery Assistance

AstroTours works with the US Coast Guard to establish a zone specifically for the rocket to splashdown and to resolve safety for other boaters to yield. The splashdown locations are very specified, as our team will select an exact date and time for each location.

Once AstroTours establishes the best location based off weather and distance, 3 Kevlar parachutes and tight airbags will deploy and will gradually slow the descent speed of the crew capsule.

Once the crew capsule splashdowns on the landing site, multiple NASA speedboats check the capsule integrity and if there are any presence of hazards such as propellant vapors.

The main vessel will hoist the capsule onto the main deck and open the metal hatch once all precautions are met.
