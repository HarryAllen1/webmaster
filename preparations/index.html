Practice

To prepare our astronauts for the zero-gravity experience they will encounter in space, we provide detailed training experiences. Within our micro-gravity Boeing plane, astronauts will learn how to navigate and interact with objects. This will help the astronauts seamlessly adapt to the weightless environment inside their spaceship.

Physical Fitness

Physical strength is a crucial aspect for all AstroTours astronauts. Due to the zero-gravity nature of space, astronauts will need to push and pull themselves when navigating. To maintain muscular strength, all astronauts will be required to engage in strength training before their mission. We will provide state-of-the-art facilities, such as the Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory, to facilitate this training.

Rocket Functionality

We will provide 1:1 scale replicas of the crew capsules and rockets so you and your fellow crewmates can learn the different functions and procedures. In particular, you will become familiar with the various emergency procedures and communications with Mission Control.

Safety

At AstroTours, the safety of our crew is of utmost importance. Our rockets are programmed with the aid of cutting-edge technology, allowing us to continuously test and redesign better systems to guarantee protection to the crew.
