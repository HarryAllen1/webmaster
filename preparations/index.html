Practice

The rockets will go up to space, which does cross over Earth’s gravitational pull. Astronauts are trained to move, play with objects, and have fun in a micro-gravity Boeing plane. This could help astronauts adapt to the new weightlessness environment inside the crew capsule during the flight.

Physical Fitness

All AstroTours astronauts must have strong muscles to lift, move, bend, and carry objects in space. To maintain muscular strength, all astronauts will be required to engage in core activities before and during the missions. This will be trained with labratories such as the Neutral Buoyancy Labratory.

Rocket Functionality

We have provided 1:1 scale replicas of the crew capsules and the rockets so that you and your fellow crewmates can learn about its different functionalities and procedures. You will specifically learn different emergency procedures, communications with Mission Control and more.

Safety

Human safety remains a large priority to designing our 3 AstroTours rockets. Our rockets are programmed autonomously by computers, allowing us to continuously test and redesign better capabilities and systems that will not cause any severe damage to the crew. Through AstroTours’ AstroNet Test Program, we have mobilized the rocket-especially the crew capsule- to land safely in case of an emergency or a system malfunction.

At AstroTours, we have created multiple backup escape systems to ensure the rockets will land safely back on the launchpad after being ejected from the capsule in space. Although a lot of test rockets have failed, our team has improvised every one of them and worked towards a successful solution to resolve the issue.

Should the rocket detect an unwanted issue in the capsule, all three rockets can eject themselves from the booster. The system has been tested and implemented thoroughly in the rocket to activate safely in any stage of flight.
